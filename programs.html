Institutional Offerings

Academic Programs.

Four diploma modules, delivered through classroom instruction, clinical labs, and supervised internship — each one built to leave you job-ready.

Diploma I

Caregiving Fundamentals

Core skills every caregiver needs: hygiene, mobility support, basic first aid, and daily client care routines.

  • Personal hygiene & grooming assistance
  • Safe moving & handling technique
  • Basic first aid & emergency response
Diploma II

Elderly & Dementia Care

Specialized techniques for supporting aging clients, including memory care and safe mobility assistance.

  • Dementia & memory-loss support
  • Fall prevention & mobility aids
  • Companionship & routine care
Diploma III

Home Health Aide Certification

Preparing caregivers to work confidently and independently inside private client households.

  • Vital-signs monitoring
  • Medication reminder protocols
  • Household safety standards
Diploma IV

Food Hygiene & Nutrition

Safe food handling, storage, and nutrition planning for clients with special dietary needs.

  • Safe food storage & handling
  • Special dietary requirements
  • Meal planning for clients
How It's Delivered

Three Parts, One Program

Classroom

Lecture Sessions

Small cohort classes where instructors walk through caregiving theory before students move to practical labs.

Clinical Lab

Practical Sessions

Guided practice on real caregiving equipment, from mobility aids to vital-signs monitoring.

Mobile

Study on WhatsApp

Diploma coursework and reading materials delivered directly to your phone, fitting around work and family life.

Your Path

From First Class to Certified Caregiver

01

Certificate

Full caregiving curriculum across 16 course modules, with guided clinical placement.
02

Diploma

Advanced technique and nanny training, building on your Level 1 certification.
03

Higher Diploma

Proficiency and leadership skill for on-site staff training and coordination roles.
04

Licensing

For qualified individuals with prior training, lifesaving certification, and completed clinical hours.
